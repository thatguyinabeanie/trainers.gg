Navigation: 4 tabs

Each tab is a distinct concern. No overlap, no dead ends.

Home

Glanceable overview. Stats, active actions, recent activity. The "what's going on right now" view. What you see when you first land.

Alts & Teams

The core management tab. Create/manage alts, view per-alt analytics, see teams owned by each alt, team usage history. The identity + strategy hub.

History

Tournament history + achievements + competitive record. Your progression over time. The "look back" tab. Also exists on public profile, this is the private management view.

Settings

Account, social identity (linked accounts, Bluesky), display preferences, notification preferences. Auto-redirects to Profile sub-tab (no dead end).

Why these 4? "Home" replaces "Overview" — friendlier name for a control center. "Alts & Teams" elevates alts from a flat list to a rich management view with analytics and teams. "History" consolidates tournament history + achievements (two paths to same data). "Settings" absorbs social identity and notification config. "Invitations" and "Notifications" fold into the Home tab as action items / inbox.

Design Decisions

Inbox replaces Notifications

Not just alerts — it's where tournament invitations, match results, action items, and system messages live. Urgent items (team submissions, active matches) surface at the top with red dots. Non-urgent below.

Two paths to the same data

Teams accessible from Alts tab (alt → its teams) AND from History tab (tournament → team used). Tournament history accessible from History tab AND from Alts tab (alt → its tournaments). No dead ends.

Aggregated + per-alt analytics

Home tab shows aggregated stats across all alts. Alts & Teams tab shows per-alt breakdowns. Both are important — aggregate tells you how you're doing overall, per-alt tells you how each identity is performing.

Settings auto-redirects

Clicking Settings always lands on Profile sub-tab. No more "Select a settings tab to continue" dead end. Sub-tabs: Profile, Account, Display, Notifications (inbox preferences).
