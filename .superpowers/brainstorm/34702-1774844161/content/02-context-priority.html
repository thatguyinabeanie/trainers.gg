Which layer surfaces first?

If the dashboard does everything, the question becomes: what rises to the top based on context?

Right now the dashboard has a "mode" system — it shows different things based on whether you're in an active match, preparing for a tournament, post-tournament, or idle. That's a good instinct. But the modes currently only change one section. What if the entire page adapted?

A

Context-Adaptive (Smart Priority)

The page reflows based on what's happening. Active match? Match hero at top, stats compressed. Tournament tomorrow? Check-in and team submission front and center. Nothing happening? Your trainer card, stats, and discovery take over. Same components, different priority order.

B

Fixed Layout, Live Content

The layout stays the same — you always know where things are. But the content within each section changes. Stats always top-left, actions always top-right, activity always below. Predictable, scannable, reliable. Think of it like a sports app where the layout is consistent but scores update live.

C

Hybrid: Fixed Skeleton + One Adaptive Zone

Most of the page is fixed (stats, identity, nav). But there's one prominent "hero" area at the top that adapts — showing an active match, an urgent action, or your trainer card depending on context. Best of both: reliability + urgency.
