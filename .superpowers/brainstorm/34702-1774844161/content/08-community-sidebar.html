How does the community section fit in the sidebar?

The sidebar needs to handle: player dashboard + community management + live tournament ops. Here are two approaches.

A

Expandable communities in the sidebar

Each community is a collapsible group in the sidebar. Sub-items (Overview, Tournaments, Staff, Settings) appear when expanded. Active tournament shows inline with a live indicator.

Strengths

  • Full hierarchy visible — always know where you are
  • Active tournaments with live dot + judge badge count in sidebar
  • Can jump between communities without losing context
  • Tournament manage tabs stay in content area (not sidebar)
  • "Request a Community" via + button in section header

Weaknesses

  • Sidebar can get long with multiple communities expanded
  • 3+ levels of nesting (community → tournaments → active tournament)
  • Collapsing sidebar to icons loses the community hierarchy
B

Context-switching sidebar

The sidebar adapts based on where you are. Player dashboard shows player nav. Clicking a community replaces the sidebar content with that community's nav. A "back" link returns to the player view. Like how Discord/Slack switch between workspaces.

Strengths

  • Clean separation — player context and community context never compete for space
  • Discord/Slack mental model — users already know this pattern
  • Community sidebar has room for "Live Now" and "Recent" tournament sections
  • Icon rail works perfectly when collapsed — each community is one icon
  • Green dot on community icon shows live tournament at a glance
  • Scales well — 10 communities is just 10 icons in the rail

Weaknesses

  • Two sidebar columns eats more horizontal space (~252px total)
  • Switching between player dashboard and community is a context switch, not just scrolling
  • More complex to implement (icon rail + contextual sidebar)
💡 My recommendation: Option B. The Discord/Slack workspace pattern is a natural fit here. A community leader might be staff in 5+ communities — Option A's expandable list would become unwieldy. Option B's icon rail scales elegantly. The green live dot on the community icon is an instant signal. And when the sidebar collapses, the icon rail IS the collapsed state — it maps perfectly to the collapsible sidebar you asked for.
