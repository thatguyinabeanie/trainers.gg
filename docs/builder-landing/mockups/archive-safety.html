Which safety model?

A

Archive (keep-but-hide) + Undo toast on delete  ·  my lean

Archive tucks a team into a 🗄 Archived view you can restore from anytime — for stuff you want to keep but not see. Delete stays permanent, but a short Undo toast catches accidents. Two clear intents (keep vs gone), with a net on the dangerous one. Works as a bulk action too.

B

Trash / recycle bin (auto-purge after ~30 days)

No separate Archive — Delete sends to a Trash that's recoverable for ~30 days, then auto-empties. One familiar concept (Drive/Gmail). But everything you remove lingers in Trash, and there's no distinct "keep long-term, out of my way" state.

C

Archive only — Delete stays a confirm dialog

Add 🗄 Archive; keep Delete behind a "Are you sure?" confirm, permanent, no undo/trash. Simplest to build. But a confirmed delete is gone for good — one mis-click past the dialog and it's lost.

My lean is A: archive for "keep but hide," and an Undo toast so a stray Delete isn't fatal — without a whole Trash subsystem to manage. Also part of D (no debate, just building): 💀 loading skeletons, ⚠️ fetch-error + retry states, and 💾 remembered UI prefs (last alt/folder/sort/density/rail).
