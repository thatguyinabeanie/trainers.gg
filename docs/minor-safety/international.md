## 🇦🇺 Australia

### Online Safety Act & Social Media Age Restrictions

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Social media minimum age** | 16 (effective December 10, 2025) |
| **Affected platforms** | Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, Threads, TikTok, Twitch, X, YouTube, Kick, Reddit |
| **Penalty** | Up to **AUD $49.5 million** |
| **Full implementation deadline** | September 2026 |
| **Independent review** | Required within 2 years of December 10, 2025 |

### Age Assurance Industry Codes

- Legally enforceable codes backed by AUD $49.5 million penalties
- Require platforms to take **reasonable steps** to prevent under-16s from creating or keeping accounts
- Cover social media platforms specifically — not all websites

### Applicability to trainers.gg

The social media minimum age law **specifically targets named platforms**, so trainers.gg would not be directly covered by the social media age restriction. However:

- The broader **Online Safety Act age assurance codes** could apply if trainers.gg includes social features (chat, messaging, profile sharing)
- The platform should implement age verification for Australian users as a precaution
- If AT Protocol/Bluesky integration classifies the platform as having "social media features," additional scrutiny may apply

### Recommendation

Implement age verification for Australian users (under 16 for social features, under 13 for account creation at minimum). Monitor the evolving regulatory landscape as age assurance codes develop.

## 🇧🇷 Brazil

### LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados)

Brazil has the **strictest age threshold globally** — requiring parental consent for processing personal data of anyone under 18.

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Child definition** | Under 12 |
| **Adolescent definition** | 12-18 |
| **Consent required** | For **all minors under 18** |
| **Consent type (under 12)** | Specific and prominent consent from at least one parent/guardian |
| **Best interest standard** | All processing of children's/adolescents' data must be in their **best interest** |
| **Verification** | Reasonable efforts using available technology |

### Key Requirements

1. **All minors under 18** — Brazil requires parental consent for all processing activities for individuals under 18, not just online services. This is broader than COPPA (under 13) or GDPR (13-16).

2. **Specific and prominent consent** — For children under 12, consent must be:
   - Specific to the data being collected
   - Prominently presented
   - Given by at least one parent or legal guardian

3. **Best interest of the child** — All processing must be carried out in the child's/adolescent's best interest

4. **Transparency** — Controllers must provide clear, accessible information about data collection purposes, adapted for children

5. **Exceptions** — Children's data may be collected without consent only to:
   - Contact a parent/guardian (used once, not stored)
   - Protect the child (not shared with third parties)

### Digital ECA (September 2025)

Brazil enacted the "Digital Statute of the Child and Adolescent" (Digital ECA) on September 17, 2025:

- Establishes a pioneering framework for protecting children and adolescents online
- Additional requirements beyond LGPD
- Imposes obligations on platform operators regarding minor safety

### Applicability to trainers.gg

If serving Brazilian users, trainers.gg must:
- Obtain parental consent for **all users under 18** (not just under 13)
- Implement age verification
- Provide Portuguese-language privacy notices
- Comply with both LGPD and Digital ECA requirements

## 🇰🇷 South Korea

### Key Requirements

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Consent age** | Under **14** requires parental consent |
| **Age verification** | Resident registration numbers used for verification |
| **Shutdown law** | Abolished August 2021 (previously blocked under-16 gaming midnight-6AM) |
| **Current system** | "Choice permit" — parents/guardians arrange approved play times |
| **Governing law** | PIPA (Personal Information Protection Act) |

### PIPA Requirements

- Children under 14 require parental consent for personal data processing
- Age verification is expected via resident registration numbers across online gaming, social media, e-commerce
- 2023: KCC imposed corrective orders on entities that didn't obtain consent from children under 14 for location information processing
- Recent PIPA amendments strengthen protections for children's data

### Applicability to trainers.gg

South Korea has a **large competitive Pokemon community**. If the platform serves Korean users:

- Must obtain parental consent for users under 14
- Implement age verification (resident registration number system or equivalent)
- The abolished shutdown law is no longer a concern
- Consider Korean-language privacy notices

## 🇯🇵 Japan

### Current State (APPI)

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Current child-specific provisions** | None in APPI |
| **De facto age threshold** | Under 15-16 — minors without capacity to assess consequences of consent |
| **Legal representatives** | Can make requests on behalf of minors (under 18 per Civil Code) |
| **Planned amendments** | Draft law anticipated in 2025, taking effect ~2027 |

### Key Points

- The APPI (Act on the Protection of Personal Information) currently has **no specific child provisions**
- However, businesses should obtain consent from legal representatives for children aged 12-15 and younger who are considered minors without capacity to assess consequences of consent
- Legal representatives can make requests for disclosures, corrections, and deletions on behalf of minors

### Nintendo/Pokemon Company Precedent

Despite the lack of specific child provisions in Japanese law:
- Nintendo and The Pokemon Company follow **ESRB Privacy Certification** for Japanese and global operations
- This provides a de facto compliance framework that exceeds local requirements
- **No known enforcement actions** against Nintendo or The Pokemon Company for children's privacy violations

### Planned Changes

APPI amendments are expected to include new children's data regulations:
- Draft law anticipated in 2025
- Taking effect approximately 2027
- Expected to align more closely with international standards

### Applicability to trainers.gg

Japan has a **massive Pokemon competitive community**. While APPI lacks specific child provisions now:

- Best practice: obtain parental consent for users under 16 (aligning with upcoming amendments and global best practices)
- Follow the Nintendo/Pokemon Company model of voluntary ESRB certification
- Monitor APPI amendments for new requirements

## 🇨🇦 Canada

### PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)

| Key Fact | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Under 13** | Parental consent required in all but exceptional circumstances |
| **13-17** | Organizations must consider child's level of maturity |
| **Sensitivity** | Children's data viewed as **particularly sensitive** |
| **Quebec** | Under **14** (not 13) |

### Key Requirements

1. **Under 13:** Consent must be obtained from parents or guardians in all but exceptional circumstances

2. **13-17:** Organizations must consider the child's level of maturity in developing consent processes

3. **Data minimization:** Youth-centric services must explain the necessity of any personal information collected

4. **Meaningful consent:** Processes must be adapted for the maturity level of the user

### Provincial Variations

| Province | Child Age Threshold | Notes |
|----------|:-------------------:|-------|
| Federal (PIPEDA) | Under 13 (strict) | 13-17 adapted consent |
| Quebec | Under 14 | Provincial privacy law |
| Alberta | Similar to federal | Own privacy legislation |
| British Columbia | Similar to federal | Own privacy legislation |

### Developments in 2025-2026

- **May 2025:** OPC launched exploratory consultation on developing a **children's privacy code** under PIPEDA
- **June 2025:** Bill C-216 (Promotion of Safety in the Digital Age Act) reintroduced — would impose best-interest-of-the-child obligations on platform operators
- **Ongoing:** OPC intends to draft children's privacy code following consultation

### Applicability to trainers.gg

Canada has an active Pokemon competitive community. The platform must:
- Obtain parental consent for users under 13 (under 14 for Quebec users)
- Adapt consent processes for 13-17 year olds
- Monitor the evolving children's privacy code development
- Consider bilingual (English/French) privacy notices for Canadian users

## 📊 Global Age Threshold Summary

| Jurisdiction | Parental Consent Age | Notes |
|:-------------|:--------------------:|-------|
| 🇺🇸 **US (COPPA)** | Under 13 | Hard threshold; proposed COPPA 2.0 would extend to under 17 |
| 🇪🇺 **EU (GDPR)** | 13-16 (varies) | See [gdpr-minors.md](gdpr-minors.md) for full country table |
| 🇬🇧 **UK** | Under 13 (consent) / Under 18 (Code) | Children's Code applies to all under-18 services |
| 🇦🇺 **Australia** | Under 16 (social media) | Broader codes may apply to other services |
| 🇧🇷 **Brazil** | **Under 18** | **Strictest globally** — all processing for all minors |
| 🇰🇷 **South Korea** | Under 14 | For personal data processing |
| 🇯🇵 **Japan** | Under 15-16 (de facto) | No specific APPI provision yet; amendments expected ~2027 |
| 🇨🇦 **Canada** | Under 13 (strict) / 13-17 (adapted) | Quebec uses under 14 |

### Safest Global Approach

> Treat all users under **16** as requiring parental consent. Apply additional restrictions for **under-13** users to satisfy COPPA. Apply broadest restrictions for **under-18** users when serving Brazilian users or when under the UK Children's Code.
